Chapter 2: Breadboard Basics

📌 WHAT IS A BREADBOARD?

A breadboard is a board with holes where you can plug electronic parts without soldering.

Simple definition: It is like a LEGO base plate — you can build and remove parts easily.

Real-life example: When you want to test a circuit, you plug parts into a breadboard. If it doesn't work, you unplug and try again. No glue, no soldering!

📌 WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

Breadboards are important because:

  • You can test circuits without soldering
  • You can change the design quickly
  • It saves money — reuse the same board!

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: Breadboard has rows of holes connected inside
  • Step 2: Put electronic parts into the holes
  • Step 3: The inside connections join the parts together
  • Step 4: Add battery and your circuit works!

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • Breadboard = Test board (no solder)
  • Like LEGO — build and remove
  • Rows are connected inside
  • Test before you build final!

Chapter 4: PCB Design Basics

📌 WHAT IS A PCB?

PCB means Printed Circuit Board. It is a board with copper lines that connect electronic parts.

Simple definition: It is like a road map made of copper for electricity to travel.

Real-life example: Look at the inside of a broken toy or old phone. You will see a green board with shiny lines and parts soldered on it. That is a PCB!

📌 WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

PCB design is important because:

  • It makes circuits small and neat
  • You can make many copies easily
  • It is strong and lasts a long time

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: Draw your circuit on a computer (schematic)
  • Step 2: The computer turns it into a PCB layout
  • Step 3: Copper lines are printed on a board
  • Step 4: Parts are soldered onto the board!

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • PCB = Printed Circuit Board
  • Copper lines like roads for electricity
  • Green board with shiny lines
  • Computer helps design it

📝 SIMPLE NOTES:

  • PCB = Circuit board
  • Copper lines = Roads
  • Green board (usually)
  • Solder parts on top

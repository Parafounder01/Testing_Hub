Chapter 5: Testing Your Circuit

📌 WHY DO WE TEST CIRCUITS?

Testing makes sure your circuit works correctly and safely.

Simple definition: It is like checking your homework before you give it to the teacher.

Real-life example: A multimeter is a tool that checks if electricity is flowing. You touch its probes to your circuit — it tells you if the circuit is good or has a problem!

📌 WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

Testing is important because:

  • It finds mistakes before they cause damage
  • It saves money — fix now, not later
  • It makes sure the device is safe to use

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: Use a multimeter to check voltage
  • Step 2: Check if electricity flows where it should
  • Step 3: Find and fix any problems (like loose wires)
  • Step 4: Test again — make sure it works!

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • Testing = Checking your work
  • Multimeter = Tool for checking
  • Find mistakes early!
  • Test, fix, test again

📝 SIMPLE NOTES:

  • Test before final build
  • Multimeter = Check tool
  • Find problems early
  • Save time and money

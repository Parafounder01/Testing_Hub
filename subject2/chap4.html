Chapter 4: Switches and Relays

📌 WHAT ARE SWITCHES AND RELAYS?

A switch is a button that turns electricity ON or OFF. A relay is a switch that uses a small electricity to control a big electricity.

Simple definition: They are like water taps — turn the tap to let water flow or stop.

Real-life example: The light switch at your home is a switch. When you press it UP, the light turns ON. A relay is like a servant who presses the switch for you when you are far away!

📌 WHY ARE THEY IMPORTANT?

Switches and relays are important because:

  • They let us control when a device turns ON
  • Relays let small signals control big machines
  • Every light and fan at home uses a switch!

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: A switch connects or breaks the circuit
  • Step 2: When connected, electricity flows → device ON
  • Step 3: When broken, electricity stops → device OFF
  • Step 4: A relay uses a small electricity to move the switch

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • Switch = Turn ON/OFF
  • Like a water tap for electricity
  • Relay = Small electricity controls big
  • Home light switch = Simple switch

📝 SIMPLE NOTES:

  • Switch = ON/OFF button
  • Relay = Remote switch
  • Like a water tap
  • Home lights = Switches

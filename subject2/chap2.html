Chapter 2: Basic Components

📌 WHAT ARE BASIC COMPONENTS?

Basic components are small parts that go inside electronic circuits.

Simple definition: They are like LEGO blocks — you put them together to build something.

Real-life example: A resistor is like a narrow pipe for water — it slows electricity down. A capacitor is like a small water tank — it stores electricity for later!

📌 THREE BASIC COMPONENTS:

  • Resistor: Slows down electricity (like a narrow pipe)
  • Capacitor: Stores electricity (like a small battery)
  • Inductor: Resists changes in electricity flow

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: Resistor limits how much electricity flows
  • Step 2: Capacitor saves some electricity for later use
  • Step 3: Inductor keeps electricity flowing smoothly
  • Step 4: Together, they make the circuit work right!

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • Resistor = Slows electricity (narrow pipe)
  • Capacitor = Stores electricity (water tank)
  • Inductor = Keeps flow smooth
  • Like LEGO blocks for circuits

📝 SIMPLE NOTES:

  • Resistor = Slows flow
  • Capacitor = Stores power
  • Inductor = Smooth flow
  • All = Circuit building blocks

Chapter 3: Antenna Basics

📌 WHAT IS AN ANTENNA?

An antenna is a stick or rod that sends and catches microwaves.

Simple definition: It is like a hand that throws and catches waves.

Real-life example: Look at the roof of a house — you will see a TV antenna. It catches TV signals from the air and sends them to your TV. Your phone also has a tiny antenna inside it!

📌 WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

Antennas are important because:

  • It lets your phone catch signals
  • It brings TV channels to your home
  • Without antenna, no WiFi or mobile phone would work

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: The antenna is made of metal (like copper)
  • Step 2: When microwaves hit it, the metal vibrates
  • Step 3: The vibration becomes electricity
  • Step 4: Your phone or TV changes electricity into picture and sound

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • Antenna = Metal stick for waves
  • It sends AND catches microwaves
  • Like a hand throwing and catching a ball
  • Metal vibrates when waves hit it

📝 SIMPLE NOTES:

  • Antenna = Metal rod
  • Sends waves OUT
  • Catches waves IN
  • Like a hand for signals

Chapter 1: What is Microwave?

📌 WHAT IS MICROWAVE?

Microwave is a type of wave. It is like light, but you cannot see it.

Simple definition: Microwave is a wave that carries signals through the air.

Real-life example: When you use WiFi at home, microwave waves carry the internet signal to your phone. Your mobile phone also uses microwave waves to talk to the tower.

📌 WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

Microwave is important because:

  • It helps your phone connect to the internet
  • It lets you talk to friends far away
  • It heats your food in the microwave oven

📌 HOW DOES IT WORK?

Let me explain in simple steps:

  • Step 1: A device (like your phone) sends out a microwave wave
  • Step 2: The wave travels through the air, like throwing a ball
  • Step 3: Another device (like a WiFi router) catches the wave
  • Step 4: The wave carries information like a letter in the mail

🔁 LET'S REMEMBER:

  • Microwave is a wave you cannot see
  • WiFi and mobile phones use microwaves
  • Waves travel through air to carry information
  • Like a ball thrown from one person to another

📝 SIMPLE NOTES:

  • Microwave = Invisible wave
  • Used in: WiFi, Mobile, Oven
  • Travels through air
  • Carries information like a letter
